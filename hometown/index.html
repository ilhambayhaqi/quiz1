Sidoarjo

Sidoarjo is a regency of East Java, Indonesia. Sidoarjo Regency is bordered by Surabaya city and Gresik Regency to the north, by Pasuruan Regency to the south, by Mojokerto Regency to the west and by the Madura Strait to the east. It has an area of 602.14 kmÂ², making it the smallest regency in East Java. As at the 2010 Census, Sidoarjo Regency had a population of 2,191,489; the latest official estimate (as at mid 2019) is 2,266,533. The regency is part of the urban region surrounding Surabaya, known as 'Gerbangkertosusila' area.

Sidoarjo is one of the developing cities in East Java that has some potentialities from industry to trade, and from a business center to tourism. It is one of the Surabaya's supporting cities, popularly known as the city of shrimp. It is no wonder that the gem, just the south of the capital, has been visited by a lot of people - besides, the hot mud tragedy boosts its popularity in the entire Indonesia. After the media's exposure, it has likely that "hot mud city" is properly named addressed to the municipal place within the area of Kertosusilo gate.
